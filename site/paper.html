Elementary Exponential Density Bounds for Collatz Growth Chains via First-Multiple-of-Four Decomposition

Hamed Mohammadpour

Abstract

We introduce the First-Multiple-of-Four (FMF) map, which replaces the classical Collatz iteration with a single algebraic step that jumps directly to the next multiple of four. Every odd integer \(x = 4k+3\) with \(k\) odd and \(k+1 = 2^t m\) reaches its FMF image in exactly \(3 + 2t\) Collatz steps, yielding the closed form \(F(x) = 2(3^{t+2}m - 1)\).

From this we derive exact transition probabilities, prove state-independence of the induced Markov chain, and establish the Quartering Law: the probability that a growth chain continues for at least \(k\) steps is exactly \(2/4^k\). This gives natural density bounds on the exceptional set with exponential \((1/4)^k\) decay, strictly stronger than Tao's logarithmic-density sub-polynomial bounds.
