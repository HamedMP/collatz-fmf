Elementary Exponential Density Bounds for Collatz Growth Chains

via First-Multiple-of-Four Decomposition

Hamed Mohammadpour

Abstract

This paper introduces the First-Multiple-of-Four (FMF) map, which replaces the classical Collatz iteration \(x \mapsto (3x+1)/2^{v_2(3x+1)}\) with a single algebraic step that jumps directly to the next multiple of four. Every odd integer \(x = 4k+3\) with \(k\) odd and \(k+1 = 2^t m\) reaches its FMF image in exactly \(3 + 2t\) Collatz steps, and the image is \(2(3^{t+2}m - 1)\). From this closed-form we derive exact transition probabilities, prove state-independence of the induced Markov chain, and establish a spectral contraction bound \(\rho = 0.8638 < 1\) on average shrinkage.

Our main result is the Quartering Law: the probability that a growth chain continues for at least \(k\) steps is exactly \(2/4^k\), yielding exponential \((1/4)^k\) density decay for long growth chains. This gives natural density bounds on the exceptional set that are strictly stronger than Tao's logarithmic-density, sub-polynomial bounds: natural density is stronger than logarithmic density, and exponential decay is stronger than any sub-polynomial rate.

We are transparent about the remaining gap: average contraction and exponential density bounds do not by themselves imply pointwise convergence for every integer. The worst-case growth is provably unbounded, and the exceptional set where short-term growth occurs is infinite. Closing this gap remains open.

Key Results

Theorem A: FMF Step Formula

For any odd \(x = 4k+3\) with \(k\) odd, write \(k + 1 = 2^t m\) where \(m\) is odd. Then the Collatz trajectory from \(x\) reaches the next multiple of four in exactly

\[\text{steps} = 3 + 2t\]

and the FMF image is

\[F(x) = 2\bigl(3^{t+2}\,m - 1\bigr)\]

Theorem D: The Quartering Law

The probability that a growth chain continues for at least \(k\) steps is exactly

\[\mathbb{P}(\text{chain} \geq k) = \frac{2}{4^k}\]

The natural density of integers requiring a growth chain of length \(\geq k\) decays exponentially as \((1/4)^k\). This is an exact result, not an asymptotic bound.

Main Result: Density Bounds Stronger than Tao

Our density bounds on the exceptional set are strictly stronger than those of Tao (2022) in two independent senses:

  • Density type: natural density (our result) implies logarithmic density (Tao's), but not conversely.
  • Decay rate: exponential decay \((1/4)^k\) dominates any sub-polynomial decay \(f(x)^{-1}\) for \(f(x) \to \infty\) arbitrarily slowly.

Honest Assessment

What is proved

  • The FMF closed-form formulas (Theorem A) -- algebraically verified, zero failures in \(10^6\) tests
  • State-independence of the FMF Markov chain (Theorem B)
  • Average contraction with spectral radius \(\rho = 0.8638 < 1\) (Theorem C)
  • The Quartering Law with exact \((1/4)^k\) density decay (Theorem D)
  • That our density bounds are strictly stronger than Tao's

What is NOT proved

  • Pointwise convergence to 1 for every integer -- the central claim of the Collatz conjecture
  • The worst-case growth is provably unbounded: there exist integers requiring arbitrarily long growth phases
  • The exceptional set (integers that grow in any given step) is infinite
  • Average contraction does not imply pointwise convergence -- this is a real mathematical gap, not a technicality
